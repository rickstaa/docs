# ComfyUI – Full Global Model Support (Not Livepeer-Specific)

This page answers **one question only**:

> **What models can ComfyUI run, in principle, regardless of Livepeer support?**

It lists **all known model families commonly used with ComfyUI**, with a clear flag indicating **whether and why each may or may not be runnable on Livepeer**.

Nothing here implies Livepeer support.

---

## Legend

- ✅ **Likely runnable on Livepeer** (fits real-time / GPU-worker constraints)
- ⚠️ **Conditionally runnable** (depends on latency, VRAM, orchestration, or batching)
- ❌ **Not suitable for Livepeer** (design mismatch, stateful, CPU-bound, or non-deterministic)

---

## 1. Diffusion Models (Image / Video)

### Stable Diffusion Family

- Stable Diffusion 1.4 / 1.5 / 2.0 / 2.1 — ✅
  _Text-to-image and image-to-image diffusion models that generate images by iterative denoising; foundation of most ComfyUI workflows, including frame-by-frame video processing._
- SDXL (Base / Refiner / Turbo) — ⚠️ (VRAM + latency)
  _Higher-capacity Stable Diffusion variant with improved composition and detail; Turbo variants trade quality for speed._
- SD-Turbo / LCM / Lightning — ✅
  _Accelerated diffusion variants designed to converge in very few steps, enabling low-latency generation._
- Kandinsky — ⚠️ (less optimized, heavier)
  _Diffusion-based image generation model emphasizing semantic understanding and text–image alignment._
- DeepFloyd IF — ❌ (multi-stage, extreme VRAM)
  _Cascaded multi-stage diffusion system using large frozen text encoders for very high-fidelity images._

**Why blocked (if any):** VRAM pressure, multi-stage graphs, inference latency

---

### Video Diffusion Models

- StreamDiffusion — ✅
  _Real-time diffusion architecture that reuses latent states across frames, enabling live video generation._
- Stable Video Diffusion (SVD) — ⚠️ (latency, frame buffering)
  _Video diffusion model that generates short coherent clips by modeling temporal consistency across frames._
- AnimateDiff — ⚠️ (temporal batching)
  _Adds motion modules to Stable Diffusion to create animated sequences from text or images._
- VideoCrafter — ❌ (offline batch generation)
  _Offline video generation model designed for batch synthesis rather than interactive use._
- Wan / Wan 2.x — ⚠️ (VRAM, unclear real-time guarantees)
  _Emerging video and world-style diffusion models that generate coherent scenes over time._
- Gen-2–style world models — ❌ (stateful, non-deterministic)
  _Stateful world-generation models that maintain long-term scene memory and internal simulation state._

**Why blocked:** temporal state, batch-only execution, non-real-time

---

## 2. Control & Conditioning Models

### ControlNet

- Canny — ✅
  _Edge-detection conditioning that guides diffusion using detected outlines._
- Depth — ✅
  _Uses estimated depth maps to guide structure and spatial consistency in generated images._
- OpenPose — ✅
  _Extracts human body, hand, and facial keypoints from images or video._
  _Conditions diffusion on human skeletal keypoints for pose-aware generation._
- Normal / Lineart / Scribble — ✅
  _Structural ControlNets that guide diffusion using surface normals, line drawings, or sketches._
- Tile / Inpaint — ⚠️ (memory)
  _Enable tiled generation or region-specific editing to handle large images or partial modifications._
- Multi-ControlNet compositions — ⚠️ (GPU saturation)
  _Combine multiple ControlNets simultaneously to enforce several structural constraints._

### T2I / I2I Adapters

- IP-Adapter — ✅
  _Injects image-based style or identity guidance into diffusion models without retraining._
- Reference-only adapters — ✅
  _Lightweight adapters that bias generation toward a reference image._
- Style adapters — ✅
  _Adapters specialized for transferring artistic or visual style._

---

## 3. Encoders, VAEs, and Latents

- SD VAEs — ✅
  _Standard variational autoencoders used to encode/decode images into latent space._
- Custom / fine-tuned VAEs — ⚠️ (memory)
  _Non-standard VAEs that can improve quality but increase VRAM usage._
- Latent consistency decoders — ✅
  _Decoders optimized for fewer diffusion steps and faster convergence._

---

## 4. Vision Models (Non-Diffusion)

### Detection / Segmentation

- YOLO (v5–v8) — ⚠️ (CPU/GPU mix)
  _Real-time object detection models that identify and localize objects in images or video._
- SAM / SAM2 — ⚠️ (VRAM, latency)
  _Segmentation models that generate pixel-level masks for objects or regions._
- GroundingDINO — ⚠️
  _Vision–language detection model that links text prompts to detected objects._
- Detectron2 — ❌ (CPU-heavy, batch-oriented)
  _Research framework for detection and segmentation, optimized for offline or batch workloads._

### Depth / Geometry

- MiDaS — ✅
  _Monocular depth estimation model inferring depth from a single image._
- DepthAnything — ✅
  _High-quality depth estimation model optimized for speed and accuracy._
- ZoeDepth — ⚠️
  _Depth estimation model focused on higher precision at increased compute cost._

---

## 5. Face, Pose & Human Models

- OpenPose — ✅
- DensePose — ⚠️ (heavy)
  _Maps image pixels to a 3D surface representation of the human body._
- Face parsing — ⚠️
  _Segments facial regions such as eyes, mouth, hair, and skin._
- FaceSwap / ReFace models — ❌ (legal + state)
  _Replace or modify faces in images or video, often involving identity and legal constraints._
- Motion transfer (First Order Motion Model) — ❌ (temporal state)
  _Transfers motion from a driving video to a source subject using temporal state._

---

## 6. Audio & Music Models

- Audio diffusion (Riffusion-style) — ⚠️
  _Generates audio or spectrograms using diffusion techniques._
- MusicGen — ❌ (long-running, batch)
  _Text-to-music generation model producing full musical clips._
- Bark / TTS models — ❌ (stateful, streaming mismatch)
  _Text-to-speech and voice generation models with long context windows._

**Why blocked:** long context windows, non-frame-based execution

---

## 7. Multimodal & VLMs

- CLIP — ✅
  _Joint image–text embedding model used for scoring, ranking, and guidance._
- BLIP / BLIP-2 — ⚠️
  _Vision–language models for image captioning and visual question answering._
- LLaVA — ❌ (LLM state + latency)
  _Multimodal model combining vision encoders with large language models for reasoning._
- Qwen-VL — ❌
  _Vision–language model integrating image understanding with LLM reasoning._

---

## 8. LLMs (Text-Centric)

- LLaMA / Mistral / Qwen — ❌
  _Autoregressive large language models for text generation and reasoning._
- GPT-style decoders — ❌
  _Transformer-based text generation models requiring token-by-token state._

**Why blocked:** token streaming, memory residency, orchestration mismatch

---

## 9. 3D / NeRF / World Models

- NeRF — ❌ (offline training)
  _3D scene reconstruction models that learn continuous radiance fields from images._
- Gaussian Splatting — ❌ (stateful scenes)
  _3D rendering technique using learned Gaussian primitives to represent scenes._
- DreamFusion — ❌ (multi-hour jobs)
  _Uses diffusion guidance to optimize 3D representations over long-running jobs._

---

## 10. Utility / Pre/Post Models

- ESRGAN / Real-ESRGAN — ⚠️
  _Image super-resolution models that upscale images while preserving detail._
- GFPGAN — ⚠️
  _Face restoration model that enhances facial detail in low-quality images._
- Background removal (U2Net) — ⚠️
  _Salient object detection model used to remove or isolate backgrounds._

---

## Core Takeaway

ComfyUI can orchestrate **almost any PyTorch model**, but:

- Livepeer favors **stateless, frame-based, deterministic inference**
- Long-running, stateful, or batch-only models are **fundamentally incompatible**
- Real-time video imposes **hard physics limits**, not software ones

This list is exhaustive at the **model-family level** and intentionally conservative.
